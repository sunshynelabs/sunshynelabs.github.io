CHECK VENTILATION – Support Guide

This article explains what the CHECK VENTILATION message means on your EC Model 1 electric dryer, what you can safely check yourself, and when to stop using the machine and call a professional.

What the message means

The dryer constantly measures airflow, temperature, and cycle behavior. When air can no longer move out of the drum as expected, the system raises a CHECK VENTILATION alert.

Common causes include:

  • Lint filter clogged or not seated properly
  • Vent outlet on the exterior wall blocked by lint or debris
  • Exhaust duct crushed, kinked, or pushed too far behind the dryer
  • Long or complex vent runs that have not been cleaned for an extended time
Important: Poor airflow can increase drying time, waste energy, and in severe cases create a fire risk. Treat this message seriously.

Step 1 – Basic checks you can do yourself

Before calling anyone, perform these simple, low-risk checks:

  • Clean the lint filter. Remove the filter, clean it completely, and reinstall it correctly.
  • Check the outside vent hood. From outside your home, make sure the vent flap can move freely and is not blocked by lint, dust, or nests.
  • Look behind the dryer. Confirm the exhaust duct is attached, not crushed or sharply bent, and that nothing is pressing the dryer tightly against the wall.

After these checks, run a small test load. If drying time returns to normal and the message does not reappear, you can continue normal use.

Step 2 – When to stop and call a vent professional

Stop using the dryer and have the vent system professionally inspected if:

  • The CHECK VENTILATION message returns repeatedly, even after basic cleaning.
  • Clothes are still damp after normal cycles.
  • The top or sides of the dryer feel unusually hot.
  • You notice a burning smell or very hot, humid air around the unit.

In these cases, contact a qualified vent cleaning service. Ask them to inspect and clean the entire duct run from the back of the dryer to the outside vent hood.

Do not continue running back-to-back cycles when airflow is restricted. This does not fix the problem and may increase risk. Resolve the vent issue first.

How ExCortex communicates with you

Your dryer includes ExCortex, an offline AI system that monitors its own behavior and can explain what it sees using simple SMS messages.

  • Messages are sent via text only – no app, no Wi-Fi, no cloud.
  • ExCortex can remind you to clean the filter or describe what it is noticing.
  • For serious airflow problems, it can tell you to stop using the dryer and schedule a vent cleaning.
  • It cannot browse the web or contact services on your behalf – you choose who to call.

This keeps control in your hands: the machine reports what it sees; you decide what to do next.

When to contact appliance service

Once you know the vent path is clear and professionally cleaned, contact appliance service if:

  • CHECK VENTILATION still appears with a clean, verified vent system, or
  • The dryer will not start or stops mid-cycle, even with good airflow.

In your service request, mention that vent cleaning has already been completed so technicians can focus on sensors, controls, or other internal components.
