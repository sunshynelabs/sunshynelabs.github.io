EC Model 1 – Terms of Service & Limited Warranty

Division: Example Corporation – Legal & Support
Appliance: EC Model 1 Electric Dryer
Effective Date: Date of original purchase or installation

This document defines the binding terms between the owner (“User”) and Example Corporation (“Manufacturer”) for use, operation, servicing, and warranty coverage of the EC Model 1 electric dryer. By installing or operating this unit, the User agrees to all terms below. This document supersedes all prior representations, verbal or written.

1. Warranty Scope

Example Corporation provides a limited manufacturer’s warranty covering internal manufacturing defects only for a period of one (1) year from the date of purchase.

Coverage applies only if the failure is proven to be inside the appliance and is not related to installation conditions, environment, maintenance failure, or external airflow restrictions.

Examples of potentially covered internal failures include:

  • Airflow sensing module failure
  • Thermistor malfunction
  • High-limit thermostat failure
  • Internal wiring defect
  • Blower motor failure
  • Control board malfunction
  • Sensor circuit failures

Coverage is conditional and only applies after all external causes have been excluded through required diagnostic procedures. Unauthorized modification or misuse of the unit voids all warranty coverage.

2. Exclusions – What Is Not Covered

The following conditions are not covered under any warranty. Responsibility for these remains with the User.

  1. Venting issues: restricted, damaged, crushed, bent, excessive-length, or improperly routed vents of any kind.
  2. Prohibited duct materials: use of flexible foil or plastic/vinyl ducts.
  3. Installation and environment: incorrect installation, lack of proper electrical grounding, absence of required ventilation, or building design issues affecting airflow.
  4. Electrical supply problems: voltage irregularities, circuit overload, or other issues originating from the building’s electrical system.
  5. Maintenance failures: not cleaning the lint filter, not cleaning moisture sensors, or not ensuring the external vent hood can open freely.
  6. Foreign objects or buildup: coins, nails, debris, or other items inside the drum or ventilation system.
  7. Cosmetic damage after installation: scratches, dents, and normal wear.
  8. Damage from misuse, abuse, or neglect: including water exposure or fire caused by external restrictions.
  9. Non-residential use: use in commercial, rental, or shared/multi-unit settings.
  10. Unauthorized repair: work performed by non-authorized service providers or with non-approved parts.
  11. External events: acts of nature, power surges, or structural failure.

3. Required Venting Standards

The EC Model 1 requires a low-resistance, unobstructed airflow environment to operate safely.

Prohibited duct materials:

  • Flexible foil ducting
  • Plastic or vinyl accordion ducting

These materials are not permitted due to their tendency to create airflow restriction, heat buildup, and fire risk.

Permitted materials:

  • Rigid smooth-wall metal ducting
  • Semi-rigid metal ducting
  • Vent runs with minimal bends and maximum straight length
  • Unobstructed external exhaust point

If the venting system does not meet these standards, the unit may display a safety code and restrict operation. This is considered normal protective behavior, not a defect.

4. Required Diagnostic Test (Duct-Disconnected Test)

Before any internal defect can be claimed, the following test must be performed and documented by the User.

Procedure

  1. Turn the dryer off.
  2. Pull the unit approximately 2–3 feet from the wall.
  3. Disconnect the exhaust hose from the back of the dryer.
  4. Leave the exhaust outlet fully open to the room.
  5. Run an empty cycle on Manual / Timed Dry for 30 minutes.

Interpretation

  • If the safety code disappears, the issue is confirmed to be external. No warranty service will be authorized. Correction of the venting system is the User’s responsibility.
  • If the safety code persists, an internal failure may exist. The User may then contact Support to request further evaluation.

This test is mandatory. If it is not performed, service authorization may be denied.

5. User Requirements Before Service Request

Before contacting support, the User must complete the following:

  • Clean the lint filter completely.
  • Clean the moisture sensors with a soft cloth and alcohol.
  • Verify that the external vent hood opens freely and is not obstructed by debris, nests, or snow.
  • Complete the Duct-Disconnected Test described in Section 4.
  • Ensure the unit is fully accessible and not blocked or stacked.

Failure to complete these steps may invalidate a service request.

6. Technician Limitations

Example Corporation technicians are authorized to service the appliance only. They do not perform work outside the unit itself.

Technicians do not:

  • Repair or replace ductwork.
  • Clean home vent systems.
  • Move, unstack, or disassemble stacked appliances.
  • Modify or repair building electrical wiring.
  • Perform structural or construction work of any kind.

Any requirement beyond the appliance connection points is the responsibility of the User.
